Rethinking Oncology Care.

I worked as part of a team of 3 designers, partnering with a pharmaceutical company to reimagine the relationship between oncologists, patients and the industry - through field research, co-design and a suite of human-centred solutions.

As part of a multidisciplinary team, we conducted an in-depth study - speaking with oncologists and internal stakeholders, mapping the full experience and designing solutions to address the gaps we found.

Note: Some details have been abstracted and the brand is kept confidential due to NDA constraints.

Context

The
Brief

A pharmaceutical company wanted to go beyond the drug. The relationship between oncologists, patients and the industry had stopped evolving - still mediated almost entirely by sales representatives, with little room for genuine knowledge-sharing or patient support.

We were brought in to understand why and to design what could come next.

Research

Into

We followed a Human-Centred Design approach - speaking directly with oncologists and internal stakeholders, and spending time observing real clinical environments.

Discovery

What
We Found

Five findings shaped every decision that followed.

01

Appointments are short and overloaded

In 15 minutes, 50% of a doctor's time is consumed by recording symptoms - leaving little room for actual care.

02

Constant learning, no time to do it

Oncologists need to stay updated - but information is dispersed across sources and mixed with content that isn't relevant.

03

Training needs go beyond the clinical

Emotional management, communication, statistics, team leadership, mindfulness - none of it is covered, all of it matters.

04

Digital era, but no data to decide with

Interviewees consistently reported having no reliable data to support better clinical decisions.

05

The industry relationship needs to evolve

Sales representatives are almost the only point of contact between pharma and doctors. Both sides know this isn't enough - but the system hasn't changed.

Define

From Insight
to Direction

We synthesised findings into three artefacts that kept the whole team grounded throughout.

Experience Map - From consultation preparation to what happens between appointments — mapped end-to-end to find where friction was highest.

Personas - Built from direct observation and real quotes. Each persona captured a distinct user - from the time-pressured oncologist to the one aiming for specialised follow-up.

Ideation workshop - Marketing, medical, and legal in the same room. Solutions built together are harder to argue with later.

Design

Three Solutions,
One Vision

Research converged on three complementary directions — each addressing a distinct gap in the oncology experience.

Patient Companion App

Simplified symptom logging between appointments. Doctors send trusted content directly. Patients feel less isolated — and arrive with better, more continuous data.

Patient App

HCP Platform

Patient input transformed into clinical trends and scores. Curated, evidence-based content surfaced at the right moment. Less admin, better decisions.

HCP Platform

Editorial Programme

A daily stream of specialty-relevant content — research, masterclasses, podcasts — delivered in formats that fit into a doctor's actual life.

Testing & Validation

Simulator
Testing

I built the prototypes used for usability testing with ~15 riders in a motorcycle simulator. The sessions focused on three areas: navigation intuitiveness, glanceability under realistic riding conditions, and system consistency across all interface states.

Results validated the core interaction model - particularly the customisation system, which riders found immediately understandable. Feedback from testing fed directly into the final iteration of the priority matrix and shortcut logic.

Key Learnings & Impact

What I Learned

This work established the structural foundation for BMW Motorrad's instrument cluster - system architecture, behaviour rules, and safety logic that will scale across motorcycles launching in 2027.

01

System thinking over individual screens

The flowchart became the single source of truth. In safety-critical interfaces, inconsistency isn't just bad UX, it's a safety issue.

02

Document while building, not after

Maintaining flowcharts and matrices throughout the process forced clearer thinking about edge cases and caught conflicts when they were still easy to fix.

03

Automotive constraints teach precision

Fixed hardware, unpredictable sensors, and safety standards taught me to think ahead. Every state needs a fallback. Every error needs clear communication.
